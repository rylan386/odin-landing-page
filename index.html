What is Coffee?

Coffee is a dark-colored, bitter, slightly acidic beverage that has a stimulating effect on humans, owing to the caffeine content. It is one of the world's most popular beverages and can be prepared and served in a variety of ways (e.g., espresso, French press, caffè latte, or already-brewed canned coffee). Coffee is normally served hot, but cooled or iced coffee is also popular. To mask the bitterness or increase the flavor, sugar, sugar substitutes, milk, or cream are frequently applied. It goes well with coffee cake or a sweet dessert such as doughnuts. A coffeehouse or coffee shop is a commercial establishment that sells prepared coffee beverages.

Different types of drinks

Caffè Americano is a coffee drink made by diluting espresso with hot water, giving it a similar strength to standard brewed coffee but a distinct flavor. Its intensity varies depending on the quantity of espresso shots used and the amount of water used.

Caffè latte is an Italian coffee beverage made with espresso and heated milk. The chocolate-flavored mocha is one variation, as is replacing the coffee with a different drink base such masala chai (spiced Indian tea), mate, matcha, turmeric, or rooibos; alternative types of milk, such as soy milk or almond milk, are also used.

A cappuccino is an espresso-based coffee beverage made with steamed milk foam that originated in Austria and was later developed in Italy.

Caffè macchiato, often known as espresso macchiato, is an espresso coffee beverage containing a little amount of foamed milk. Macchiato means "stained" or "spotted" in Italian, therefore caffè macchiato literally means "stained" or "marked coffee."
